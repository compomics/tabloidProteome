Frequently Asked Questions

What is Tabloid Proteome?

Tabloid Proteome is protein association network generated using publically available mass spectrometry based experiments in PRIDE. These associations includes; protein-protein interaction, complex formation, or different role in single pathway.

How all these associations are calculated?

The association between two proteins are calculated using our method (Gupta et al. under revision) which uses Jaccard Similarity score to calculate the value for association. Our method is based on the assumption that consistent protein co-occurrence across a large collection of different, unrelated MS experiments is not merely due to chance, but that this instead reveals an underlying biological association between these co-occurring proteins. We have also included the biological relation between the protein pairs using five existing knowledgebases; IntAct and BioGRID for interaction, CORUM for complex, Ensembl for paralogs, and Reactome for pathways.

Is it free to use?

Yes, Tabloid Proteome data are available for free.

How can I download data and in which formats?

You can download your search result in tab delimited format, from Data Table view. You can also download the image of the Graph provided in graph view. You can change nodes/edges position and download as image., in .png format.

Does it provide all interacting partners of protein from IntAct and BioGRID?

No, Tabloid Proteome version 1.1 only contains 2325 protein pair, found to be associated according to our method. Other information from the different knowledgebases are added to provide user with existing biological information.
